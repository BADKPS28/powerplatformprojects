⚙️ Technical
Do I need a Microsoft 365 licence to use Power Platform?+
Most Power Platform products require at least a Microsoft 365 licence, though specific features need Power Apps or Power BI Premium licences. I'll assess your current licences in the discovery call and recommend the most cost-effective setup — often you already have what you need.
How long does a typical project take?+
A Starter project (single app or flow) typically takes 2–3 weeks. A Professional project (full process) takes 4–6 weeks. Enterprise engagements are ongoing. I always provide a detailed timeline in the proposal before work begins.
What's included in the fixed price?+
Discovery, design, build, testing, deployment, and handover documentation are all included. The only extras are Microsoft licensing costs (which go directly to Microsoft), and optional ongoing support after the included support period ends.
Can I start with a small project and scale up?+
Absolutely — this is the most common path. Many clients start with a Starter project to prove ROI internally, then commission a larger Professional or Enterprise engagement once stakeholders see the results.
Will my team be able to maintain it after delivery?+
Yes — this is a priority. Every project includes handover documentation and a training session. I build solutions using best-practice naming conventions and patterns so any Power Platform developer can pick it up. I also offer post-launch support retainers.
Can Power Platform connect to our existing systems?+
Power Platform has 800+ built-in connectors including Salesforce, SAP, Dynamics 365, SQL Server, Oracle, and most major SaaS platforms. For custom or legacy systems, I can build custom connectors or use Azure Functions as a bridge.
